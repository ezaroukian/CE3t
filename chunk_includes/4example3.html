Rules can also contain variables.

  • We will use the variables P1 and P2 to vary over the people John, Mary, and Peter.
  • We will use the variables B1 and B2 to vary over the books War and Peace, Middlemarch, and Moby-Dick.

Here the rule is: if (the person P1 has as name Peter) then (the person P1 reads the book War and Peace).

Remember that P1 can be any of the people John, Mary, and Peter.

To determine if the diagram is consistent with this rule, we need to find the cases in the diagram where the 'if'-clause is true, then check whether the 'then'-clause is true.

  • IF: The 'if'-clause is true when P1=Peter (the person Peter has as name Peter)
  • THEN: When P1=Peter, the 'then'-clause (the person Peter reads the book War and Peace) is true

The diagram is therefore consistent with the rule.
